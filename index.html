New kinds of social coordination

Silicon Valley startups are all about the 'sharing economy'. Books starting with Clay Shirky's Here Come Everybody, and most recently Paul Mason's Postcapitalism all describe new, digitally facilitated social structures.

Big corporates are opening 'innovation lofts' and coworking spaces, trying to work out what the AirBnB of washing powder is.

Many of these phenomena are examples of social coordination: organising people and resources to achieve collective goals. Historically, there have been two prominent solutions: markets and governments.

But when digital information is so abundant, hybrid, or even completely new, kinds of social coordination can emerge. Not all digital phenomena are about social coordination. I didn't include Udacity, the eduction website, because it seems like a digital version of conventional university. I did include Peer To Peer University, because it has a fresh approach to generating educational material, a new way of connecting supply and demand.

Below, you can see various projects organised according to three different : Resource Structure, Exchange Method, and Spare Capacity. Most of the projects are concrete examples of digital social coordination, some are thrown in because they're informative.
