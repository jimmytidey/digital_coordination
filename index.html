New kinds of social coordination

Social coordination is the problem organising people and resources to achieve collective goals. Historically, there have been two prominent solutions: markets and governments.

Ideas like the sharing economy, open source software, and Wikipedia all seem to herald new ways of doing things.

Sometimes they're new ways of making government more effective, sometimes, they're new kinds of markets, and sometimes they seem to be something completely different - voluntary, reputation based, even working on barter.

Are these phenomena really connected, and if so how? This pages explores three ways of understanding these new, mostly digital phenomena, with some old ways thrown in for reference.
